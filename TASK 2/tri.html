Savitri

Indian Actress

Here's a time line of Savitri life

Early life
  • 6 December 1935: Nissankara Savitri was born to Nissankara Subhadramma, Guravayya. In a Telugu-speaking family in Chirravuru, Guntur district of present-day Andhra Pradesh. Her father died when she was six months old.
  • 1942: After which her mother took Savitri and an older sibling Maruti, to live with an aunt and uncle. Her uncle Kommareddy Venkata Ramayya Chowdary, enrolled her in classes when she began to show a talent for dance.
  • 1948: She is too intelligent girl from her childhood only.Everyone will get attracts to her beautiful eyes. She used to play dramas and small small acting in her village. Savithri and her uncle went to Madras (studio) for opportunity to play roles in movies.
  • 1950: The next day they went to another studio, Gemini Ganesan take her photos and placed in some magazines. After one year she got chance in movies.she was cast as the female lead in Samsaram in 1950.
  • 1952: Savitri married Tamil actor Gemini Ganesan having first met him in 1948. Other names :- Mahanati Savitri Nadigaiyar Thilagam
  • 1950-1981: Savitri started acting at the age of 13, she got married at 16, rose to superstardom in her 20s, turned alcoholic in her 30s and died in her 40s. It was truly one of the most dramatic and extreme lives led by an actor in Indian cinema.
Films
  • 1950: She was spotted by LV Prasad, the director of Samsaram. It was released on 29 dec 1950 she acted as a junior artist.
  • 1951: She played a role as a dancer in a film called Patala Bhairavi .
  • 1952: Savitri's first significant role was in the 1952 Tamil-Telugu bilingual film Pelli Chesi Choodu which simultaneously made in Tamil as Kalyanam Panni Paar
  • 1953: She has starred in successful ventures such as Devadasu which received special mentions at India International Film Festival
  • 1955: Donga Ramudu And she also starred in works such as Missamma , Ardhangi
  • 1957: Mayabazar , Thodi Kodallu
  • 1959: Mangalya Balam
  • 1962: Aradhana Gundamma Katha
  • 1963: Nartanasala Is featured at the Afro-Asian film festival in Jakarta.
  • 1964: Doctor Chakravarty.
  • 1965: Sumangali and Devata.
  • 1981: Harischandrudu
Awards
  • 1953: She won the Best Actress for the film Devadasu
  • 1957: She won the Best Actress for the film Mayabazar
  • 1960: She won the Best Actress for the film Chivaraku Migiledi
  • 1962: She won the Best Actress for the film Aradhana
  • 1970: She won the Rashtrapati Award for the film Chivaraku Migiledi
  • 1968: She won the Nandhi Award for the film Best Feature Film: Chinnari Papalu
Death
  • 1981: Savitri died on 26 December 1981, at the age of 47, after being in a coma for 19 months. She had developed diabetes and high blood pressure.
Additional-Info
  • Savitri was also noted for her kindness, charity, and generosity towards the poor. Savitri received "A Moon Among Stars" honor at the 30th International Film Festival of India, "Woman in Cinema" section in 1999. The 2018 biographical film Mahanati based on the life of Savitri won the "Equality in Cinema Award" at the 2018 Indian Film Festival of Melbourne. In 2011, The Government of India issued a postage stamp in commemoration of Nissankara Savitri. Nissankara Savitri Ganesan's biopic, titled Mahanati, starring Keerthy Suresh as Nissankara Savitri and Dulquer Salmaan as Gemini Ganesan was released in 2018 to critical acclaim and commercial success.
